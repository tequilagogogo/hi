About May 17

May 17 is the International Day Against Homophobia, Transphobia and Biphobia, an annual landmark to draw the attention of policymakers, opinion leaders, social movements, the public and the media to the violence and discrimination experienced by lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, intersex people and all other people with diverse sexual orientations, gender identities or expressions, and sex characteristics.

History

The date of May 17 was specifically chosen to commemorate the World Health Organization's decision in 1990 to declassify homosexuality as a mental disorder. Since then, May 17 has become a global celebration, with events happening in more than 130 countries, including 37 where same-sex acts are illegal.

Activities

Events to celebrate May 17 include marches, parades, rallies, cultural performances, workshops, and educational sessions. These events aim to raise awareness, promote tolerance, and advocate for the rights of the LGBT community.
